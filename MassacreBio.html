Biography

The Birthday Massacre (abbreviated TBM) is a Canadian rock band, formed in 1999 in London, Ontario, and currently based in Toronto, Ontario. The current lineup consists of lead vocalist Chibi, rhythm guitarist Rainbow, lead guitarist Falcore, drummer Rhim, keyboardist Owen, and bassist Nate Manor.

When the band formed in 1999, they were known as Imagica. This name was inspired by the fantasy novel Imajica (1991) by Clive Barker. In 2002, they changed the name to The Birthday Massacre after one of their earlier songs in order to avoid confusion with another group. The song "The Birthday Massacre" was then renamed to "Happy Birthday".

Chibi

Chibi is the lead singer and songwriter of The Birthday Massacre. She was born in Canada on April 26, 1977 and was one of the key founders of the band 1999. Her stage name, Chibi, was inspired by the Sailor Moon character, Chibi Moon.

Rhim

Rhim is a Toronto based musician, best known for his roll as the drummer for TBM. He joined the band in 2003, but has also played in Fallon Bowman, The Grace Dynasty, and has recorded with the Toronto rock band Aphasia.

"It kind of works well for the music that we're making. Sort of contrasty, you know? Birthday, and massacre. Light, and dark. Cute, and evil."

- Chibi

Rainbow

Rainbow is the band's rythm guitarist and also does backing vocals, audio engineering, mixing, producing, and drum programming. He and Chibi were classmates in a Fine Arts Program at Fanshawe College in London, Ontario, when they decided to start a band together.

Falcore

Falcore is a guitarist, song writer, keyboardist, and synth drum programmer for The Birthday Massacre. He was studying video production in Toronto before they decided to start TBM.
